# ZEIGEN — A Prefix Guide for English Speakers

## The Stem

**Zeigen** means to show, to point, to indicate. It implies directing attention — making something visible or apparent to someone else. Hold this image in mind: a finger pointing at something and saying "look." Every prefix below reshapes that pointing gesture — redirecting it, formalizing it, or intensifying it.

---

## The Prefixes

### AB- ("off, away, branching")

**Abzeigen:** to branch off. A rare compound. The prefix sends the pointing gesture away from the main direction — a path *zeigt ab*, it branches off, it diverges from the main line. The image is of a signpost pointing away from the road you are on.

Literal mnemonic: "point off." A branch "points off" from the trunk.

### AN- ("on, at, toward an authority")

**Anzeigen:** to point at, to indicate, to report. This is the richest prefix in the family, because *an-* directs the pointing gesture toward multiple targets. A gauge *zeigt an* — it points at a reading, it indicates. You *zeigen an* at the police — you point at a wrongdoer, you report them, you file charges. A newspaper *zeigt an* — it displays an advertisement, it announces. In every case, the prefix directs the showing toward a specific recipient or record: a dial, an authority, the public.

Literal mnemonic: "point at" or "show to." Think of "indicating to the authorities" — the showing is directed, formal, on-the-record.

### AUF- ("up, out into the open")

**Aufzeigen:** to show up, to point out. The prefix lifts the showing gesture upward into visibility — you raise your hand to show, you bring something up for inspection, you point out a problem so others can see it. The emphasis is on making something visible that was previously unnoticed.

Literal mnemonic: "show up" or "point out." "Let me point out the issue" — *aufzeigen* lifts the hidden thing into view.

### BE- (intensifier / formalization)

**Bezeigen:** to show forth, to express formally. *Be-* stamps the showing action fully onto an object — you do not merely show but formally express, display, demonstrate. *Respekt bezeigen* — to show respect. *Teilnahme bezeigen* — to express sympathy. The prefix elevates casual showing into deliberate, formal expression.

Mnemonic: "be-" = "thoroughly, formally." *Bezeigen* is showing made ceremonial — you show something with full intent and weight.

### ER- (achievement, bringing about)

**Erzeigen:** to show oneself, to render, to bestow. *Er-* brings the showing to fruition — you show something that results in a real effect. *Einem eine Ehre erzeigen* — to render someone an honor. The prefix implies that the showing produces something: a favor bestowed, a kindness rendered. Somewhat formal and archaic.

Mnemonic: "show forth to completion." The showing has a result — something is given, rendered, accomplished through the display.

### HIN- ("thither, toward a point away from the speaker")

**Hinzeigen:** to point toward, to point out. The prefix gives direction — the pointing gesture goes away from the speaker, toward something in the distance. Simple and spatial: *zeig mal hin* — point over there, draw attention to that thing.

Literal mnemonic: "point thither." The finger points out toward a specific location.

### VOR- ("before, forward, in front of")

**Vorzeigen:** to show before, to present. The prefix places the shown object forward, in front of the viewer — you hold it up for inspection. A passport is *vorgezeigt* — shown to the officer, presented for examination. A skill is *vorgezeigt* — demonstrated, displayed before an audience. The gesture is presentational: here, look at this.

Literal mnemonic: "show forth." "Present your ticket" = *zeigen Sie Ihren Ausweis vor.* The thing is pushed forward for viewing.

## Patterns Worth Noting

A few observations for the English-speaking learner:

**The stem is about directing attention; the prefixes control where it goes.** *Zeigen* always means "make someone see." *Anzeigen* directs the seeing toward an authority. *Aufzeigen* lifts it into the open. *Vorzeigen* pushes it forward for inspection. The core gesture of pointing never changes — only its target and context shift.

**This is a small, well-behaved family.** Unlike *ziehen* or *legen*, the *zeigen* family has relatively few prefixed forms and most stay close to the literal meaning of showing or pointing. This makes it one of the easier verb families to master.

**Separable prefixes stay spatial.** *Ab, an, auf, hin, vor* — all describe a physical direction for the pointing or showing gesture. You can picture the finger (or the display) moving in each direction.

**Inseparable prefixes formalize.** *Bezeigen* and *erzeigen* are both somewhat elevated in register — formal, literary, ceremonial. *Be-* intensifies the show into deliberate expression; *er-* transforms it into a concrete bestowal. Both have moved from casual pointing into institutional language.

**English "show" parallels are close.** "Show off" ~ *aufzeigen*. "Show respect" ~ *Respekt bezeigen*. "Present" ~ *vorzeigen*. "Report/indicate" ~ *anzeigen*. The mapping between English "show" compounds and German *zeigen* compounds is fairly direct — use these parallels as footholds.
