# WETTEN — A Prefix Guide for English Speakers

## The Stem

**Wetten** means to bet, to wager. It implies staking something of value on an uncertain outcome. Unlike most verb stems in this collection, *wetten* has no prefixed variants in common use. It stands alone.

## Patterns Worth Noting

A few observations for the English-speaking learner:

**Some stems resist prefix compounding.** *Wetten* is one of the few German verb stems in this collection with no prefixed derivatives. This is partly because the meaning is already narrow and specific — "to bet" does not lend itself to spatial redirection the way "to lay," "to turn," or "to throw" does. You cannot meaningfully "bet in," "bet off," or "bet under."

**The word is cognate with English.** *Wetten* and English "wed" share a common Germanic root meaning "to pledge." A wedding is literally a pledging ceremony; a wager (*Wette*) is a pledge of money on an outcome. The connection reminds us that betting, at its core, is about making a binding promise.

**The isolation is itself instructive.** When a stem has many prefixed forms, the learner must untangle them. When a stem has none, the lesson is different: some actions are already so complete in themselves that German's prefix system finds no purchase. *Wetten* is what it is — a bet placed, nothing more to add.
