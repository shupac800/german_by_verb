# WÄHLEN — A Prefix Guide for English Speakers

## The Stem

**Wählen** means to choose, to select, to elect, to dial. It is a verb of decision — singling out one option from many. Hold this image: a hand pointing at one thing among several, or a finger turning a rotary dial. The prefixes below redirect that choosing — reversing it, refining it, repeating it, misaiming it, or setting it in advance.

---

## The Prefixes

### AB- ("off, away from, un-doing")

**Abwählen:** to choose off, to vote away. The prefix reverses the election — what was chosen is now unchosen. A politician is *abgewählt* — voted out of office, removed by ballot. A school subject is *abgewählt* — dropped, deselected from the schedule. The prefix takes the act of choosing and turns it into an act of rejection.

Literal mnemonic: "vote off." English "vote out" captures the political sense exactly. Think of "voting someone off the island."

### AUS- ("out, outward, from among")

**Auswählen:** to choose out, to select from among. The prefix sharpens the choosing — you reach into a group and pull one out. You *wählst* a candidate *aus* — you pick them out from the pool. You *wählst* a gift *aus* — you select it from the options. The prefix adds discrimination: not just choosing, but choosing out from a set after careful examination.

Literal mnemonic: "pick out." English "single out" and "pick out" carry the same force — selection from among many. *Auswählen* is the deliberate, curated version of *wählen*.

### ER- (achievement, elevated choosing)

**Erwählen:** to choose with finality, to elect solemnly. *Er-* elevates and completes the act of choosing — this is not casual selection but a weighty, often formal or even divine choice. A people *erwählt* their leader. The *auserwählte Volk* is the chosen people. The prefix adds gravity and permanence to the choosing.

Mnemonic: "choose to completion." Where *wählen* is the everyday act, *erwählen* is the grand, decisive, once-and-for-all selection. Think of "the chosen one" — that is an *Erwählter*.

### HINZU- ("toward, in addition")

**Hinzuwählen:** to choose in addition, to co-opt. The prefix adds someone to an existing group by election — not replacing anyone, but expanding the body. A committee *wählt* a new member *hinzu* — they add them by vote. The prefix combines approach (*hin*) with addition (*zu*): the new person is chosen toward the group and added to it.

Literal mnemonic: "choose in addition." "We co-opted two new members" = *Wir haben zwei neue Mitglieder hinzugewählt.*

### VER- (error, going astray)

**Verwählen:** to choose wrongly, to misdial. *Ver-* twists the choosing astray — the finger hits the wrong number, the selection goes wrong. *Sich verwählen* means to misdial, to dial the wrong number. The prefix is purely about error here: you intended to choose correctly but *ver-* sent the action off course.

Literal mnemonic: "mis-choose." English "misdial" captures the telephone sense. The *ver-* is the same "mis-" that appears in *verlegen* (mislay), *verlaufen* (get lost) — the action aims wrong.

### VOR- ("before, in advance, pre-")

**Vorwählen:** to choose before, to preselect. The prefix moves the choosing earlier in time — you select in advance, before the main event. A *Vorwahl* is a primary election (choosing before the general election) or an area code (the numbers you dial before the main number). The prefix is temporal: the choice happens first, setting the stage.

Literal mnemonic: "pre-choose." English "preselect" is the exact equivalent. A *Vorwahl* is a "pre-dial" — the code you enter before the number.

### WIEDER- ("again, anew")

**Wiederwählen:** to choose again, to reelect. The prefix repeats the choosing — the same person is selected a second time, or the same number is dialed again. A president is *wiedergewählt* — reelected. A number is *wiedergewählt* — redialed. The prefix is purely iterative: the same action, once more.

Literal mnemonic: "choose again." English "reelect" and "redial" both use "re-" where German uses *wieder-* — the logic is identical.

## Patterns Worth Noting

A few observations for the English-speaking learner:

**The stem stays decisional; the prefixes modulate timing, direction, and accuracy.** *Wählen* never stops meaning "to choose." But the prefixes create a complete grammar of choosing: choosing out (*aus-*), choosing wrong (*ver-*), choosing in advance (*vor-*), choosing again (*wieder-*), choosing away (*ab-*), choosing in addition (*hinzu-*), choosing solemnly (*er-*). Each prefix tells you something different about how, when, or how well the choosing was done.

**Separable prefixes are spatial and transparent.** *Ab-, aus-, hinzu-, vor-, wieder-* — these are all concrete in their directionality. You can picture each one: picking out from a group, voting someone off, adding by election, selecting in advance, choosing once more.

**The one inseparable prefix elevates the action.** *Er-* fuses with the verb and raises the choosing to a formal or solemn register. *Erwählen* is not casual picking but grave, deliberate election. This follows the broader pattern of *er-* as completion and achievement.

**The telephone and the ballot box share a verb.** This is distinctive about *wählen*: dialing a phone number and casting a vote are both acts of selecting from options, and German uses the same verb for both. This means the prefixes do double duty: *vorwählen* is both "preselect" and "dial an area code"; *wiederwählen* is both "reelect" and "redial"; *verwählen* is "misdial" just as it could theoretically be "misvote." Pay attention to context — but recognize that the underlying metaphor is always the same pointing finger choosing one among many.
